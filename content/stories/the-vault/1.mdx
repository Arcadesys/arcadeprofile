Maya pressed her palm against the cold steel and felt it hum.

The door shouldn't have been there. The building plans from 1923 showed a solid wall — twelve inches of reinforced concrete behind the plaster. She'd checked twice. Three times, if you counted the microfiche at the county records office, which she did, because Maya counted everything.

"You're staring at a wall again," said Dev from across the lobby.

"It's not a wall." She knocked twice. The sound came back wrong — hollow where it should have been solid, resonant where it should have been dead. "Listen."

Dev sighed but walked over. He knocked. His eyebrows went up.

"That's not a wall," he agreed.
